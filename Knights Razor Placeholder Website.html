Great choice! The animation styles of Zero Punctuation, Cyanide & Happiness, and similar simple-figure illustrations are approachable, efficient, and effective for humor, critique, and storytelling. Here's how you can develop a similar animation style—both artistically and technically:

🧠 Style Breakdown & Inspirations

🟨 Zero Punctuation (Yahtzee)

  • Stick-figure-like characters with blocky heads and simple limbs

  • Bright monochrome backgrounds (often yellow)

  • Minimal animation — characters slide, bounce, or wiggle for emphasis

  • Symbols and props (guns, swords, UI elements) appear to illustrate jokes

  • Heavy use of voice-over and timing for punchlines

🟦 Cyanide & Happiness

  • Simple black-line stick figures

  • Round heads, dot eyes, and small mouths

  • More frame-by-frame movement than ZP, but still minimal

  • Often looped or reused animations

🛠️ Tools to Use (Beginner to Intermediate)

1. Storyboard & Draw

  • Krita (Free): Great for drawing frames with timeline support

  • Clip Studio Paint (Paid): Popular among comic and animation artists

  • Procreate (iPad, Paid): Intuitive and fast for character designs

2. Animate

  • OpenToonz (Free): Used by Studio Ghibli, good for 2D workflows

  • Adobe Animate (Paid): Classic for symbol-based animation

  • Rive or Spine (Free tier): For cutout/paper-doll style rigging

  • Moho Debut (Affordable): Great for rigged 2D animation

3. Simple Video Editors

  • DaVinci Resolve (Free): Excellent for combining voiceover + animation

  • Shotcut or CapCut (Free): Simpler options for layering images/audio

🎨 Art Style: Getting Started

Characters

  • Start with vector or shape-based figures (circles, rectangles)

  • Keep limbs detachable for easy movement (think puppets)

  • Use bold outlines and flat colors

Props & Backgrounds

  • Design modular props (e.g., guns, speech bubbles, etc.)

  • Backgrounds can be solid colors or repeating patterns for simplicity

🗣️ Voice-Over Timing (ZP Style)

  • Record your script first

  • Time animations to match punchlines and emphasis

  • Add simple movement for rhythm: characters bob, jump, or slide with the jokes

📚 Tutorials to Watch

  • "How to Animate Like Zero Punctuation" (fan recreations and breakdowns)

  • "Cyanide & Happiness Animation Process"

  • "Beginner's Guide to Cutout Animation"

💡 Tips

  • Use limited animation intentionally — subtle movement can be funnier

  • Let the writing carry the comedy; animation supports it

  • Create reusable assets: arms, expressions, props — build a library
